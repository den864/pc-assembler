Motherboard

What is a Motherboard? A Motherboard is a printed circuit board and foundation of a computer and is the biggest board in the computer chassis. Motherboards are also sometimes refered to as a mb, mainboard, mboard, backplane board, base board, main circuit board, planar board, system board, or a logic board on Apple computers. There are also diffrent types of motherboards, each being designed to fit different types and sizes of computers. Motherboards do not have compatibility with every processor and memory type as each one is designed to work with specific types processors and memory. Motherboards do, however, work with a majority of hard drives regardless of the brand because hard drives are mostly universal. Motherboards also contain various components which include but is not limited to peripherals, interface cards, daughtercards (sound cards, video cards, network cards, hard drives, and other forms of persistent storage), TV tuner cards, extra USB slots, extra FireWire slots, and various other components as well.
